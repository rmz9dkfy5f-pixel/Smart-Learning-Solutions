# Content

**Status:** Active
**What belongs here:** Copy rules, tone guidelines, CTA strategy, metadata standards, and content ownership rules.
**What does not belong here:** Business strategy, design decisions, or implementation code.

---

## 1. Tone and Voice

**Tone:** Direct, confident, credible
**Voice:** Expert practitioner speaking to a concerned parent or decision-making educator
**Reading level:** Plain English accessible to a general adult audience
**Personality:** Warm but not playful; authoritative but not cold

## 2. Key Messages

1. Hands-on, in-person STEM that students remember
2. Real robotics and real science — not screen-based activities
3. Expert-led and credentialed (licensed Microbric partner; Whybricks certified)
4. Easy to book for any group size or setting
5. State-aligned lessons and activities

## 3. CTA Strategy

| Context | CTA Text |
|---|---|
| Primary booking action | "Request a Workshop" |
| Quote request | "Get a Quote" |
| Secondary / exploratory | "Learn More" |

**Never use:** "Buy Now", "Order", "Start Free Trial", "Sign Up", "Join", or any e-commerce language.
**Never imply:** Virtual delivery, subscription, or self-directed online learning.

## 4. Program Names

Do not rename or paraphrase the programs:

| Canonical Name | Platform | Ages |
|---|---|---|
| Coding with Robots | Edison (Microbric) | 5–18 |
| PSTEM | Whybricks | 10–18 |

## 5. Audience Framing

- **Primary:** Parents — lead with safety, quality, and genuine educational value
- **Secondary:** Schools and educators — lead with curriculum alignment and credibility
- **Tertiary:** Community groups — lead with flexibility and accessibility

## 6. Claims Policy

**Must not fabricate:**
- Metrics (number of students served, satisfaction rates, etc.)
- Testimonials (must be real; use placeholder if unavailable)
- Capabilities not yet confirmed
- Partner relationships not confirmed
- Awards or certifications not held

**Confirmed claims allowed:**
- Licensed Microbric partner (Edison platform)
- Certified Whybricks PSTEM provider
- Professional and certified engineers, coders, and educators
- State-aligned lessons and activities
- Serving students worldwide

## 7. Language Rules

| Rule | Reason |
|---|---|
| No virtual language | Delivery is in-person only |
| No pricing | Quote-only model |
| No "order" or "buy" | Not a product; it's a service enquiry |
| No jargon without explanation | Parents are the primary audience |
| No passive voice for CTAs | Direct and action-oriented is clearer |

## 8. Metadata Standards

Every page must have:
- `<title>` — unique per page, includes brand name, max 60 characters
- `<meta name="description">` — unique per page, 140–160 characters, includes a CTA hook
- `<meta property="og:title">` — same as or similar to `<title>`
- `<meta property="og:description">` — same as or similar to meta description
- `<meta property="og:image">` — points to `src/images/og-image.svg`
- `<link rel="canonical">` — full URL for the page

## 10. Placeholder Policy

If content is missing, use a clearly marked placeholder. Examples:
- `[TESTIMONIAL — pending owner input]`
- `[PHOTO — pending owner photography]`
- `[OUTCOME DATA — pending owner confirmation]`

Never fill a placeholder with invented content.
