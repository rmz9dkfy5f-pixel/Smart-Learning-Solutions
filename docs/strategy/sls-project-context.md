# Smart Learning Solutions — Project Context

## Overview
Smart Learning Solutions is a STEM education company that delivers hands-on, in-person workshops for K–12 students. This website is the company's primary marketing and lead-generation tool. Its sole conversion goal is getting visitors to submit a workshop enquiry.

## Mission
Help parents, schools, and community groups bring expert-led, hands-on STEM workshop experiences to the students in their care.

## Goals and Objectives
- **Conversion:** Every page should direct visitors toward requesting a workshop
- **Credibility:** The site must feel premium and expert-led — not template-like or generic
- **Clarity:** A new visitor should understand "STEM workshop provider" within 5 seconds
- **Simplicity:** Clean navigation, no dead pages, no unnecessary content

## Target Audiences
- **Parents (primary):** Booking on behalf of their child or a group of children. They want to know the experience is safe, high-quality, and genuinely educational.
- **Schools and Educators:** Teachers, curriculum coordinators, and school administrators looking for curriculum-aligned STEM enrichment. They need to know the program is credible and easy to organise.
- **Community Groups:** Youth clubs, libraries, holiday programmes, and community organisations that run activities for young people.

## Primary Offerings
1. **Coding with Robots** — Powered by the Edison robotics platform (Microbric). Students progress through four coding levels: Barcode → EdBlocks → EdScratch → EdPy (Python). Ages 5–18.
2. **PSTEM** — Powered by the Whybricks inquiry-based learning platform. Students investigate real physical science questions ("Why don't bridges collapse?", "Why do objects slow down?") through 10 hands-on investigation modules. Ages 10–18.

## Key Constraints
- No CMS — all content is in source files
- No virtual workshop language anywhere
- No pricing on the site
- No fabricated metrics, testimonials, or capabilities
- Navigation must stay lean — 7 items maximum

## What to Avoid
- Recreating old dead pages (Curriculum, Professional Development, Order)
- Teacher-resource framing that buries the booking CTA
- Generic or playful design that undercuts the premium/trust positioning
- Overloading the homepage with PDFs, downloads, or text blocks
- Any language that implies virtual delivery
