Implementation Roadmap
Stage 1
Lock the sitemap and homepage section order.
Stage 2
Write homepage messaging for each wireframe section:
* hero
* program cards
* audience section
* process
* differentiators
* CTA blocks
Stage 3
Create the visual direction:
* typography hierarchy
* spacing system
* premium palette
* image treatment
* card style
* button system
Stage 4
Build the static page set and booking flow.
Stage 5
Refine Resources so they support conversion rather than acting as the main destination.
Assumptions
I’m assuming:
* “workshop booking” is the main business outcome, above downloads or quote requests
* the two flagship offers remain Coding with Robots and PSTEM
* the site should appeal to parents, schools, and group organizers, but with a stronger “new learner” framing
* you want a hand-coded marketing site, not a large editorial content system
Risks
The main risks are:
* keeping too much of the old teacher-resource framing on the homepage
* making the design “modern” but not clearly premium
* retaining too many navigation items from the current site
* using weak or inconsistent imagery, which would undermine the polished direction
* trying to preserve every old page instead of designing around the new conversion goal
Open Questions
The next decisions that would sharpen the wireframe into page-level content are:
* Are workshops primarily in-person, virtual, or both?
* Who books most often: parents, schools, or organizations?
* Do you want pricing visible, hidden, or “starting at”?
* Do you want Book a Workshop to be a direct form or a short inquiry + follow-up process?
Guardrails
Do not:
* keep Curriculum or Professional Development in the top nav unless they gain real content
* let Resources overshadow workshop booking
* use the old “Order” framing
* overload the homepage with downloads, PDFs, or text blocks
* make the visual style look playful at the expense of premium trust
The clean next move is a section-by-section homepage content outline with sample headline/copy directions.
